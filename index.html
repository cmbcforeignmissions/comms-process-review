⑦ Registration & Payment Details
📋  A Note to the Business Manager
Currently, building paid event registration forms and generating QR codes falls on your desk alongside your core accounting and financial oversight responsibilities. This proposed process removes that burden entirely.

Under the new system, the Communications team configures the pre-built JotForm template — updating only the category names, prices, and event name, which takes approximately three minutes. You receive an automatic notification with a preview of the form, confirm the pricing is authorized, and activate payment with one click. The QR code generates itself at that moment — no manual creation, no additional steps.

Your role becomes financial approval only. We want to know if this works for you from an accounting standpoint, and whether there is anything about the current process we need to preserve before making this change.

Proposed Communications Request Flow
Once the unified form is submitted, here is exactly what happens — who gets notified, who reviews, who approves, and when it reaches the media team. Every step is automatic. Nothing routes through email.
1
Ministry Submits Request
Ministry Assistant or Liaison
Fills out the unified ClickUp intake form. One submission covers the graphic request and — if applicable — triggers the registration track simultaneously.
2
Auto-Routes to Designer
ClickUp Automation
Instantly assigned to the designer. Comms Director is notified and can intercept and take the work based on current workload. No email. No forwarding.
3
Designer Uploads Draft
Designer / Comms Director
Draft uploaded directly into the ClickUp task. Ministry assistant and leader notified automatically. All versions saved in one place.
4
Ministry Reviews On the Graphic
Ministry Assistant & / or Leader
Click directly on the image to pin a comment at the exact location. No more describing changes in text. Approve or request edits.
Edits requested → returns to designer automatically  |  Approved → routes to Communications Director
5
Comms Director Final Check
Communications Director
Brand check, correct file specs for each distribution channel, and run schedule confirmed. Passes → goes to media. Fails → back to designer only.
6
Media Team Receives Final File
Media Team
Receives only fully approved, spec-checked, brand-compliant files. Their role is production and upload only — not review.
⏰

The Only Hard Deadline: Thursday at Noon

This is an approval deadline — not a submission deadline. Requests can come in any day. What matters is that the graphic is fully approved and in the media team's hands by Thursday at Noon to run on Sunday. Missed that window? It holds until the following Sunday. This protects the media team and puts the accountability where it belongs: with the submitter.

⚡ Paid Event Registration — Parallel Track (Runs Simultaneously with Design)

Step 1F
Ministry flags payment at intake
Selects "Paid Registration" on the unified form. Enters category names and prices. That is the only thing the ministry assistant does.
Step 2F
Comms team configures JotForm
Opens pre-built template, updates category labels + prices + event name only. ~3 minutes. Everything else is locked.
Step 3F
Business Manager approves
Receives notification with form preview. Confirms pricing and activates payment. One click. No building. No form construction.
Step 4F
QR code auto-generated
Form goes live. JotForm auto-creates the QR code. Delivered to Comms Director for embed into the final approved flier before media handoff.
