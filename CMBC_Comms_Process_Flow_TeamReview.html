Standard Design Request Flow — No Payment Involved
1
Ministry Submits Request
Ministry Assistant or Liaison
Submits request in ClickUp: ministry name, graphic type, placement (Sunday slide, social, email, print), event date, copy, and whether registration is needed.
2
Routes to Designer
Automatic — ClickUp
Request auto-assigns to the designer. Communications Director is notified and can take the work directly if available. No email chain needed.
3
Designer Uploads Draft
Designer / Comms Director
Draft graphic uploaded directly into the task. Ministry assistant and leader are notified automatically that a draft is ready for review.
4
Ministry Reviews On the Graphic
Ministry Assistant & / or Leader
Reviewer clicks directly on the image to pin comments at the exact spot. No more describing changes in text. Approve as-is or request edits.
If edits requested → returns to designer automatically  |  If approved → routes to Communications Director
5
Comms Director Final Check
Communications Director
Brand standards, correct file size for format, and placement/run schedule confirmed. If it passes, it moves to media. If not, it goes back to the designer — not back to the ministry.
6
Media Team Receives Final File
Media Team
Media team receives only fully approved, correctly sized, brand-checked files. Their job here is upload and production — not review or approval.
⏰

The Only Hard Deadline: Thursday at Noon

This is an approval deadline, not a submission deadline. Requests can come in any day. What matters is that the graphic is fully approved and in the media team's hands by Thursday at Noon to run on Sunday. If it misses that window, it holds until the following Sunday — no exceptions. This protects the media team and puts accountability where it belongs: with the submitter.

Paid Ministry Event Registration — Parallel Track
When Registration or Payment Is Needed
1
Ministry flags at intakeChecks "Registration/Payment Required" in the request form. Fills in: category names, prices, open/close dates. That's it — no form building.
2
Comms team configures JotFormOpens the pre-built template, updates category labels + prices + event name only. Everything else is locked. Takes ~3 minutes.
3
Business Manager approvesReceives a notification with a preview of the form. Confirms pricing and activates payment. One click. No building required.
4
QR code auto-generatedThe moment the form is activated, JotForm creates the QR code automatically. Delivered to Comms team for embed into the final flier.
Registration Form Structure
•
Category-based pricingExample: Adults $25 / Teens $10 / Children Free. Registrant selects quantity per category — total calculates automatically.
•
Up to 4 registrants per submissionFields for Registrant 1–4 appear progressively. No blank rows shown until needed.
•
Groups larger than 4"Registering more than 4 people? Describe your group below — a ministry rep will follow up."
•
Real-time trackingMinistry leader and Business Manager can see registrations as they come in — no manual counting.
